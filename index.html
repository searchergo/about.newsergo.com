Ergo is on a mission to spark meaningful conversations by way of meaningful insights in the news. Powered by technology, Ergo uses machine learning and natural language processing to group together relevant stories and break down key contextual insights to give you the full scope of what and how news is being reported.

How does it work?

Ergo is different from a news aggregator or search engine.
Here’s a look at how Ergo takes you from one article to a full road map of that story.

Question

Let’s say you’re scrolling through a platform and see a news article that you find surprising or questionable. Search for that headline within the Ergo database.

Search

A digital fingerprint of that story is created through natural language processing and matched against hundreds of thousands of articles in the database to find stories with similar digital fingerprints.

Insight

Search results outlines all the sources and the timeline of how that story has developed. Additional context tags help you understand how reliable or how different groups are discussing a single event.

Deeper Insights

Here's how Ergo highlights deeper context so you can have the full picture.

How it's being discussed

An snapshot of the publishers who are reporting on this subject. Ergo also outlines source bias, meaning publishers who tend to report through the lense of a political ideaology. See how stories develop differently and what stories are being discussed more on the left and right sides of politics.

Who's talking about it

An aggregate of the stories being reported on a single topic. Ergo uses tags to highlight potential elements that may influence the reader such as "opinionated language", "negative tone", and "left" or "right".

Background

Stay informed on previous events or reports that influence the news you're reading today. Ergo provides links to relevant Wikipedia pages so you have the full context of a story.

Publisher Overview

Learn more about the publisher reporting on a story including any source bias that may influence their reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What media sources are in Ergo's database?

The Ergo database pulls from the News API including 50 news sources reporting over 4,000 articles a day. Ergo has articles dating back to March of 2020.

How do you determine a source's media bias?

Ergo works with Ad Fontes Media and AllSides, two non-profit news publication monitoring organization, to distinguish where a media source ranks in terms of political leaning and reliability of reporting.

Is Ergo a neutral platform?

Ergo strives to generate more transparency in the news. We understand that biases in news leads to deeper polarization while also acknowledging the challenges around establishing a truly unbiased reporting system. That is why we strive for transparency both from media sources and from our team.

What does “opinionated language” mean and how does

Ergo determine it?

ergo uses natural language processing to detect words and phrases in context that suggest an opinion. The article may also contain facts, but opinions are also expressed in the article. At this time Ergo does not distinguish an article that states opinions from an article from an opinion section of a publication.

How does Ergo determine what’s in the “background” section?

Ergo analyzes the title and description of an article to identify phrases, names, or events that support the content of the article. We pull the Wikipedia pages for these words, names or events to provide relevant context.

How does Ergo determine whether a story is opinionated?

The Ergo algorithm uses sentiment analysis to learn and label language that indicates how polarized a statement is and flags those as being opinions.

How is Ergo funded?

Concerned by the implications and threat of misinformation, Ergo was started in a bedroom out of San Francisco in mid-2019. We are a small team of 5, pursuing this as a passion project without any funding.

What do the “left” and “right” tags mean and how is it determined?

These articles are published by sources that are known to report with a strong political ideology when interpreting an event.

What does “positive” and “negative” sentiment mean

and how is it determined?

Ergo uses natural language processing to detect words and phrases that suggest the article’s overall tone.
